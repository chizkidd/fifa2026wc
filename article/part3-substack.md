## The superstars: the men carrying the weight

Every World Cup gets remembered through a handful of faces. These are the ones already wearing the crown.

**Kylian Mbappé (France)** is the best player on the planet and he's captaining the best squad in the tournament. He's already got a final hat-trick on his record — the cruel kind, the one that *lost*. He wants to win one as the man. That's a dangerous motivation.

**Lamine Yamal (Spain)** is the scariest sentence in football: he's still a teenager and he's already this good.

**Lionel Messi (Argentina)** is the defending champion and this is, almost certainly, the last dance. The body is 38. Logic says it fades. History says never write him off.

Then there's **Vinícius Júnior**, finally needing to drag Brazil somewhere deep. **Erling Haaland**, the most ruthless No.9 alive, on this stage for the very first time. **Jude Bellingham**, who saves his biggest moments for the biggest nights. **Cristiano Ronaldo**, chasing the one prize that got away. And **Mohamed Salah**, who carried Egypt here basically by himself.

> The superstars I can describe. The breakout kids are why you actually watch — because in three weeks, one of these names goes from "promising" to "global."

## The breakout kids: the next icons

- **Rayan Cherki (France)** — 22, now at Manchester City, elite vision, ready to step into the spotlight France has held open for him.
- **Nico Paz (Argentina)** — 21, a Real Madrid product lighting up Como, playing with a joy you can't coach. Even Totti is gushing.
- **Ibrahim Maza (Algeria)** — 20, already making noise at Bayer Leverkusen.
- **Arda Güler (Turkey)** — the silky left-footed jewel of a thrilling young Turkish side.
- **Gilberto Mora (Mexico)** — a teenager carrying real hype for the co-hosts.
- **Endrick (Brazil)** and **Jamal Musiala (Germany)** — one's Brazil's next great striker, the other makes Germany must-watch again.

## The hidden gems: nobody's talking about them (yet)

This is the section I love most. Players from smaller setups who finish the summer with a new club and a new price tag.

**Kerim Alajbegović** is 18 and already in the Bundesliga, carrying Bosnia & Herzegovina. **Nestory Irankunda** is the raw, thrilling Australian winger. **Simon Adingra** can unlock any defence for the Ivory Coast. **Relebohile Mofokeng** is one of African football's brightest for South Africa. **Valentín Barco** is Argentina's left-sided future. And **Hugo Sochurek** is a genuine Czech wonderkid.

Write these six down. By August, you'll want to have called them early.

## Coaches decide everything in a short tournament

Over a 38-game league season, talent wins. Over seven games in a month, the **coach** wins.

- **Carlo Ancelotti** — most decorated club coach in history, now the first foreigner to lead Brazil at a World Cup. Tournament football is his art form.
- **Marcelo Bielsa** — has remade Uruguay in his own relentless image. Box-office, win or lose.
- **Julian Nagelsmann** — rebuilding Germany around a generation of creators, and it's working.
- **Ralf Rangnick** — the godfather of gegenpressing has made Austria a team nobody wants to draw.
- **Didier Deschamps** — his last France tournament. He doesn't play pretty. He plays winning.
- **Jesse Marsch** — has given co-hosts Canada a fearless, aggressive identity.
