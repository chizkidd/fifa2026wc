Let's go.

## The superstars: the men carrying the weight

Every World Cup gets remembered through a handful of faces. These are the ones already wearing the crown.

**Kylian Mbappé (France)** is the best player on the planet and he's captaining the best squad in the tournament. He's already got a final hat-trick on his record — the cruel kind, the one that *lost*. He doesn't want to be a great player who won a World Cup as a kid passenger. He wants to win one as the man. That's a dangerous motivation.

**Lamine Yamal (Spain)** is the scariest sentence in football: he's still a teenager and he's already this good. If Spain win it — and plenty of smart people think they will — this becomes his tournament and probably his coronation as the best on earth.

**Lionel Messi (Argentina)** is the defending champion and this is, almost certainly, the last dance. The body is 38. The logic says it fades. History says never, ever write him off.

Then there's **Vinícius Júnior**, finally needing to drag Brazil somewhere deep. **Erling Haaland**, the most ruthless No.9 alive, on this stage for the very first time. **Jude Bellingham**, who saves his biggest moments for the biggest nights. **Cristiano Ronaldo**, chasing the one prize that got away, in his final attempt. And **Mohamed Salah**, who carried Egypt here basically by himself.

That's the top of the bill. Now the more interesting question.

## The breakout kids: the next icons

The superstars I can describe. The breakout kids are why you actually watch — because in three weeks one of these names goes from "promising" to "global."

- **Rayan Cherki (France)** — 22, now at Manchester City, elite vision, ready to step into the spotlight France has been holding open for him.
- **Nico Paz (Argentina)** — 21, a Real Madrid product lighting up Como, playing with a joy you can't coach. Even Totti is gushing about him.
- **Ibrahim Maza (Algeria)** — 20, an attacking midfielder already making noise at Bayer Leverkusen.
- **Arda Güler (Turkey)** — the silky left-footed jewel of a genuinely thrilling young Turkish side.
- **Gilberto Mora (Mexico)** — a teenager carrying real hype for the co-hosts. Remember the name.
- **Endrick (Brazil)** and **Jamal Musiala (Germany)** round it out — one's Brazil's next great striker, the other makes Germany must-watch again.

And **Lamine Yamal** belongs on both lists. He's already a superstar. He could leave this tournament as *the* superstar.

## The hidden gems: nobody's talking about them (yet)

This is the section I love most. The players from smaller setups who finish the summer with a new club and a new price tag.

**Kerim Alajbegović** is 18 and already in the Bundesliga, carrying Bosnia & Herzegovina's hopes. **Nestory Irankunda** is the raw, thrilling Australian winger capable of a moment from nothing. **Simon Adingra** can unlock any defence for the Ivory Coast. **Relebohile Mofokeng** is one of African football's brightest young things for South Africa. **Valentín Barco** is Argentina's left-sided future. And **Hugo Sochurek** is a genuine wonderkid in a Czech side that's short on stardust.

Write these six down. By August, you'll want to have called them early.

## Coaches decide everything in a short tournament

Here's the thing people underrate. Over a 38-game league season, talent wins. Over seven games in a month, the *coach* wins. Tactics, substitutions, who you trust at 0-0 in the 75th minute — that's the whole tournament.

So watch the touchlines:

- **Carlo Ancelotti** is the most decorated club coach in history and now the first foreigner to ever lead Brazil at a World Cup. Tournament football is his art form. If anyone can make Brazil's chaos coherent, it's him.
- **Marcelo Bielsa** has remade Uruguay in his own relentless, lung-busting image. Box-office, win or lose.
- **Julian Nagelsmann** is rebuilding Germany around a generation of creators, and it's *working*.
- **Ralf Rangnick**, the godfather of gegenpressing, has turned Austria into a team nobody wants to draw.
- **Didier Deschamps** is managing his last France tournament. He doesn't play pretty. He plays *winning*.
- **Jesse Marsch** has given co-hosts Canada a fearless, aggressive identity that makes them genuinely dangerous.

## The bracket: I told you I'd pick a winner

Right. No more hedging. Based on the group order I called in [Part 2](https://chizobao.substack.com/p/2026-world-cup-groups-preview-all), here's the whole thing.

**My twelve group winners:** Mexico, Switzerland, Brazil, Turkey, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, Spain, France, Argentina, Portugal, England.

**The quarter-finals I land on:**

- 🇫🇷 **France def. Netherlands** — the Dutch are talented and brittle; France are talented and ruthless.
- 🇪🇸 **Spain def. Czechia** — Spain's midfield turns this into a possession exhibition.
- 🇧🇷 **Brazil def. England** — the pick that'll start the most fights. England create a final-four ceiling and then bump their head on it again.
- 🇦🇷 **Argentina def. Portugal** — two ageing kings collide; Messi's machine has the better engine room.

**Semi-finals:**

- 🇫🇷 **France def. Spain** — the real final, a round early. The two best teams in the world, and I think France's depth and Mbappé edge a classic.
- 🇧🇷 **Brazil def. Argentina** — Ancelotti out-coaches the champions in the game South America has been waiting for.

**Third place:** 🇪🇸 Spain def. Argentina.

**The final, at MetLife, July 19:**

> 🏆 **France def. Brazil.** A new generation of French superstars, with Mbappé finally lifting it as *the man*, proves too strong. France are world champions again.

There it is. France. In ink.
